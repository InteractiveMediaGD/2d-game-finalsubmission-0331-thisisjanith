1. Summary of the Game Concept

Space Defender is a 2D side-scrolling space shooter developed in Unity. The player controls a spaceship that must survive against waves of incoming enemy ships and asteroids while collecting health packs to stay alive. The game progressively increases in difficulty as the speed of obstacles rises over time, challenging the player to survive as long as possible and achieve the highest score.

Key Gameplay Loop

  • Move the spaceship to dodge enemies and asteroids
  • Shoot projectiles to destroy enemies and earn points
  • Collect health packs to restore health
  • Survive as long as possible as difficulty increases over time

The game features a main menu, in-game pause functionality, a game over screen with restart and exit options, and immersive sound effects including background music, shooting sounds, blast effects on enemy destruction, and health pack pickup sounds. The player's score is tracked and displayed on the UI throughout the gameplay session. The health system provides visual feedback through a health bar that depletes as the player takes damage.

2. Core Features Implementation

2.1 Health System

The health system is managed by the HealthManager.cs script attached to the player. The player starts with a maximum health of 100, displayed as a fill bar on the UI using Unity's Image.fillAmount property. When the player collides with an enemy or asteroid, TakeDamage(20f) is called, reducing health by 20 and clamping it between 0 and the max value. When health reaches 0, the Game Over screen is displayed via GameOverManager. Health resets to full when the game restarts via the Start() method.

📄 Scripts: HealthManager.cs, GameOverManager.cs

2.2 Score System

The score is managed through a singleton ScoreManager.cs script. Score is displayed as a TextMeshProUGUI element on the UI. Players earn +10 points by passing through obstacles (via ScoreTrigger.cs) and +50 points by destroying enemies with projectiles. The score resets to 0 on game restart through the ResetScore() method called in Start().

📄 Scripts: ScoreManager.cs, ScoreTrigger.cs

2.3 Health Packs

Health packs are collidable objects spawned by the Spawner.cs script with a 10% spawn chance. When the player collides with a health pack, the HealthPack.cs script triggers Heal(20f) on the player's HealthManager, restoring 20 health (clamped to the max value). A pickup sound effect plays on collection. The health pack is destroyed immediately on pickup. If the player misses it, the health pack is automatically destroyed when it moves off-screen (x < -15f).

📄 Scripts: HealthPack.cs, Spawner.cs

2.4 Projectile Attack

The player fires projectiles using the left mouse button or spacebar, handled by PlayerController.cs. A shooting sound effect plays each time the player fires. Projectiles are instantiated at the player's fire point position and travel to the right at a speed of 20 units/second. Each projectile is automatically destroyed after 2 seconds (lifetime) or upon collision with an enemy or wall. The Projectile.cs script handles collision detection, scoring, blast sound effects, and cleanup.

📄 Scripts: PlayerController.cs, Projectile.cs

2.5 Enemy Mechanics

Enemies are spawned from the right side of the screen by Spawner.cs with a 60% spawn chance. They move leftward at a base speed multiplied by the global difficulty speed. Enemies are destroyed on collision with the player (dealing 20 damage with a blast sound) or when hit by a projectile (awarding 50 score points with a blast sound). Enemies that pass off-screen (x < -15f) are automatically cleaned up. Additionally, asteroids spawn with a 30% chance, featuring random rotation and varied speeds for visual variety, and also trigger blast sounds on collision.

📄 Scripts: EnemyController.cs, Astroid.cs, Rotator.cs, Spawner.cs

2.6 Speed Increment

The DifficultyManager.cs script manages a static globalSpeed variable that starts at 1.0 and increases by 0.05 per second. This multiplier is applied to enemy movement speed and spawn rate, making the game progressively harder. The speed is capped at a maximum of 3.0 to ensure the game remains playable. The speed multiplier resets to 1.0 when the game restarts.

📄 Script: DifficultyManager.cs

2.7 Sound & Audio System

The game features a comprehensive audio system to enhance the gameplay experience:

  • Background Music: Looping background track played via an AudioSource on the BackgroundMusic GameObject
  • Shoot Sound: Laser sound effect triggered via AudioSource.PlayOneShot() each time the player fires (PlayerController.cs)
  • Blast Sound: Explosion sound played via AudioSource.PlayClipAtPoint() when enemies or asteroids are destroyed (Projectile.cs, EnemyController.cs, Astroid.cs)
  • Health Pickup Sound: Pickup chime played via AudioSource.PlayClipAtPoint() when collecting a health pack (HealthPack.cs)

PlayClipAtPoint() is used for objects that are destroyed on impact — it creates a temporary AudioSource at the collision position that persists after the object is destroyed, ensuring the sound plays fully.

4. Creative Feature — Screen Shake

The creative feature implemented in this game is a dynamic screen shake effect that provides impactful visual feedback during gameplay collisions. The feature is managed through the CameraShake.cs singleton script attached to the main camera.

How It Works

The screen shake system uses a coroutine-based approach that temporarily offsets the camera's position from its original coordinates using randomized values within a configurable magnitude range. The effect has two parameters:

  • Duration: How long the shake effect lasts (in seconds)
  • Magnitude: How intense the shake is (the displacement range)

When It Triggers

Event Duration Magnitude Feel
Player hit by enemy/asteroid 0.2s 0.3 Heavy, impactful shake
Enemy destroyed by projectile 0.1s 0.1 Light, satisfying shake

Technical Implementation

The ShakeCoroutine runs a loop for the specified duration. Each frame, it generates random X and Y offsets within the magnitude range and applies them to the camera's position. Once the duration elapses, the camera snaps back to its original position. A StopShake() method was implemented to immediately halt any active shake and reset the camera — this is called when the Game Over screen appears to prevent the shake effect from persisting on the frozen screen.

Impact on Gameplay

The screen shake adds a visceral, physical quality to the game. The differentiation between heavy and light shakes helps the player distinguish between taking damage (bad) and destroying enemies (good), improving the overall game feel and player engagement without adding UI clutter.

📄 Script: CameraShake.cs
